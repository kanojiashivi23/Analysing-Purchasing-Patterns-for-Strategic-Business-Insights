# 📊 Analysing Purchasing Patterns for Strategic Business Insights
## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior and demographic trends for a retail company. Using transaction and customer datasets, the analysis focuses on identifying revenue drivers, customer segments, and operational inefficiencies to support strategic business decisions.

## 🎯 Business Problem

The company aims to answer key business questions:

1. Which products generate the highest revenue?
2. How do discounts and shipping costs impact profitability?
3. Do demographics (age, income, country) influence purchasing behavior?
4. Are there differences in purchasing patterns across genders?
5. How can shipping processes be optimized?

## 📂 Datasets Used
1. Purchase Data
Contains:
a. Product name
b. Price, discount, tax
c. Shipping cost & duration

2. Customer Data
Contains:
a. Age
b. Gender
c. Income
d. Country

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
a. Python
b. Pandas
c. NumPy
d. Matplotlib
e. Seaborn
f. Jupyter Notebook

## 🔍 Analysis Performed
📈 1. Revenue Analysis
~ Calculated total revenue using price, discount, tax, and quantity
~ Identified Top 5 revenue-generating products
~ Visualized product contribution using bar charts
👥 2. Customer Segmentation
~ Grouped customers into age categories
~ Visualized age distribution (Histogram & Pie Chart)
~ Identified top countries by customer count
~ Analyzed geographic customer distribution
🚚 3. Shipping Cost Optimization
~ Compared average shipping costs:
~ Orders delivered within 3 days
~ Orders delivered after 3 days
~ Identified inefficiencies in delivery timelines

## 📊 Key Insights
1. A small number of products contribute significantly to total revenue (Pareto effect)
2. Customer purchasing behavior varies across age groups and regions
3. Certain countries dominate customer distribution
4. Delayed shipments tend to increase costs, indicating optimization opportunities
5. Discounts and pricing strategies directly impact revenue generation
